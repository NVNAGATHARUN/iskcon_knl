Understanding Our Mission, Values & Spiritual Heritage

About ISKCON

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), also known as the Hare Krishna movement, was founded in 1966 by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. The society is dedicated to spreading the chanting of the holy names of Krishna and the teachings of Bhagavad-gita and Srimad-Bhagavatam as a means for spiritual enlightenment and material liberation.

ISKCON operates over 500 temples, schools and restaurants worldwide, and continues to distribute literature to millions of people seeking spiritual knowledge and a higher purpose in life.

Our Philosophy
  • Bhakti Yoga Devotional service as the path to enlightenment
  • Krishna Consciousness Loving devotion to Lord Krishna

  • Chanting the Holy Names Hare Krishna, Hare Krishna, Krishna Krishna, Hare Hare...

ISKCON Kurnool Temple

Our Mission

ISKCON Kurnool serves as a spiritual home for devotees, students and families in and around Kurnool, providing daily deity worship, kirtan, discourses and prasadam distribution. We are committed to:

  • Spreading Krishna consciousness
  • Facilitating spiritual growth
  • Building a compassionate community
  • Serving all with love and devotion
What We Offer

The temple regularly conducts programs, festivals and outreach activities following the standards and teachings of Srila Prabhupada and the wider ISKCON society:

  • Daily arati and darshan services
  • Scriptural classes and discourses
  • Youth and family programs
  • Festival celebrations and prasadam distribution

Founder-Acharya: Srila Prabhupada

His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada (1896-1977) is the Founder-Acharya of ISKCON, whose revolutionary books and teachings guide all activities of ISKCON centers worldwide, including ISKCON Kurnool. He dedicated his life to spreading Krishna consciousness to every corner of the world.

Srila Prabhupada's legacy continues through the dedicated service of thousands of devotees who strive to fulfill his mission of making Krishna consciousness available to all people regardless of their background or status.
